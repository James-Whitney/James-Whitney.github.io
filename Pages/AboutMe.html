About Me

Northwest Native

I grew up in Bellevue, Washington where I fell in love with the rain and mountains. From a young age I was engrossed with computers and video games. In elementary school I attended Digipen programing summer camps. This exposed me at a very early age to programming and game development. By middle school my interest in computers was established, I’ve always been active in trying build communities with friends around the games that we play. For example, in the sixth grade I designed and launched a simple website for my clan of friends. By my eighth grade I was hosting game and VoIP servers. I enjoyed the technical aspect of server management, always installing mods, tools, and web UIs that far outweighed the needs of our small community servers. In High school I had greater control over my academic career, which I used to push myself into as many advanced placement courses as I could. It was around this time I discovered my obsession with learning new things especially in the sciences, which was the greatest influence in my becoming an engineer.

New Graduate from Cal Poly!

In 2014 I entered Cal Poly as a General Engineering major (we were required to declare upon application), and used the flexibility of the major to explore different aspects of engineering. From hand drafting of mechanical parts to PCB layout, I dipped my hands into as many departments as I could before settling on computer engineering. Computer engineering at Cal Poly sits right between both electrical engineering and computer science, offering the best of both worlds as students within the major have access to the course catalog of both departments. This was a perfect fit for me with my long-cultivated interest in computers and programing, combined with the extensive physics and math curriculums of the electrical engineering department and the hands-on aspects of the labs. Despite only officially changing into the major at the beginning of my junior year, my early explorations into electrical and computer science classes enabled me to graduate in four years with a publication to boot.

During my final year at Cal Poly, a detour in computer graphics technical electives got me involved with Cal Poly’s computer graphics research group. I became the first manager of the new campus Mixed Reality Lab. The lab served to give students access to emerging virtual and augmented reality devices and the computers to run them. Over the course of my final year I was heavily involved with the group. Including trips to conferences and as part of my senior project, working with the group’s faculty supervisor, Christian Eckhardt. A big part of my responsibility at the lab was educating students on the current state of technologies and giving them hands-on experience with state-of-the-art hardware.

What I do for fun!

Despite being finished with school for now, my quest for knowledge has not ceased. Learning new things has always been one of the biggest joys in my life. I’m often listening to online lectures and podcasts especially when I’m running or going on hikes. I see myself as a vacuum for knowledge. While I do follow the latest advancements in computer hardware, machine learning, and computer graphics, I also really enjoy learning new things outside my discipline such as biology, architecture, and psychology.

In my spare time I enjoy experimenting with my home server and Raspberry Pis which act as an experimental platform for my tinkering with different operating systems, compute clusters, container setups, home automation tools, and self-hosted cloud services. My server is also home to many community game servers I run. Playing video and board games with friends is one of my favorite pastimes. I play a lot of games, typically of the building/simulation genre, but my all-time favorite game is Minecraft, with a bunch of mods. I always have an expert mode modpack running on my server at home that I return to when I need a break from the world.
